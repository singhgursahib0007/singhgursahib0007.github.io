# Research: Attachment Styles

**Date:** 2026-04-14
**Target Video Length:** 8 minutes

## Key Facts & Findings

### Origins of Attachment Theory
- Fact: John Bowlby published "Forty-Four Juvenile Thieves" in 1944, finding that emotional disorders and delinquency could be traced to early attachment-related experiences — specifically separations from or inconsistent/harsh treatment by caregivers.
  - Source: Bretherton, I. (1992). The origins of attachment theory: John Bowlby and Mary Ainsworth. Developmental Psychology.
  - Link: https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1993-01038-001
- Fact: Bowlby proposed that early attachment experiences form mental representations called "internal working models" — cognitive-affective schemas about the self as worthy/unworthy of care and others as reliable/unreliable sources of support. These models operate automatically and unconsciously throughout life.
  - Source: Simply Psychology — Internal Working Models of Attachment
  - Link: https://www.simplypsychology.org/internal-working-models-of-attachment.html
- Fact: Bowlby's "canalization hypothesis" suggests that as relationships progress, individuals' attachment working models become increasingly stable and resistant to change over time.
  - Source: Wikipedia — Internal Working Model of Attachment
  - Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internal_working_model_of_attachment

### Ainsworth's Strange Situation Experiment
- Fact: Mary Ainsworth developed the "Strange Situation" procedure, observing 100 middle-class American infants (12-18 months) through a series of interactions involving caregivers, strangers, and brief separations.
  - Source: Simply Psychology — Mary Ainsworth
  - Link: https://www.simplypsychology.org/mary-ainsworth.html
- Fact: Ainsworth identified three primary attachment styles; Main and Solomon later added a fourth (disorganized). Distribution: ~65% secure, ~20% avoidant, ~10-15% anxious/ambivalent, ~5-10% disorganized.
  - Source: Wikipedia — Strange Situation
  - Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strange_situation
- Fact: Inter-rater reliability for the Strange Situation classifications is ~94%, demonstrating strong reliability.
  - Source: Simply Psychology — Mary Ainsworth
  - Link: https://www.simplypsychology.org/mary-ainsworth.html

### Adult Attachment and Romantic Love
- Fact: In 1987, Hazan and Shaver published a landmark paper showing that romantic love can be conceptualized as an attachment process, and that the relative prevalence of the three attachment styles is roughly the same in adulthood as in infancy.
  - Source: Hazan, C., & Shaver, P. R. (1987). Romantic Love Conceptualized as an Attachment Process. Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 52, 511-524.
  - Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3572722/
- Fact: Self-reported romantic attachment pattern was related to beliefs about love, mental models of self and social relationships, and recollections of early experiences with parents.
  - Source: Hazan & Shaver (1987)
  - Link: https://adultattachmentlab.human.cornell.edu/HazanShaver1987.pdf

### Neuroscience of Attachment
- Fact: Neuroimaging shows that affective evaluation is decreased in avoidantly attached individuals but increased in anxiously attached individuals. Attachment style influences encoding of approach vs. aversion tendencies, implicating amygdala, hippocampus, striatum, insula, and cingulate cortex.
  - Source: Vrtička, P. & Vuilleumier, P. (2012). Neuroscience of human social interactions and adult attachment style. Frontiers in Human Neuroscience.
  - Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3398354/
- Fact: The central amygdala activation by social punishment is modulated by anxious attachment style — higher anxiety scores correlate with stronger amygdala response.
  - Source: Vrtička & Vuilleumier (2012)
  - Link: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2012.00212/full

### Anxious Attachment and Protest Behaviors
- Fact: Anxious attachment is strongly associated with higher jealousy, elevated fear/sadness/anger in jealousy-provoking situations, and increased monitoring of partner behavior.
  - Source: PMC — The Impact of Romantic Attachment Styles on Jealousy in Young Adults
  - Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10659228/
- Fact: Protest behaviors in anxiously attached individuals include clinging, emotional escalation, and provocative actions to draw partners closer — these are indirect attempts to resolve attachment disruption.
  - Source: Attachment Project — Protest Behavior and Attachment
  - Link: https://www.attachmentproject.com/love/protest-behavior-and-attachment/

### Disorganized Attachment and Trauma
- Fact: Disorganized attachment is the most extreme and unpredictable of the four styles, stemming from intense fear as a result of childhood trauma, neglect, or abuse. The caregiver is simultaneously the source of comfort and fear.
  - Source: WebMD — Disorganized Attachment Style in Adults
  - Link: https://www.webmd.com/mental-health/disorganized-attachment-style
- Fact: Disorganized attachment mediates the link from early trauma to externalizing behavior in adult relationships (study of 510 adults).
  - Source: Woodhouse, S. et al. (2015). Disorganized attachment mediates the link from early trauma to externalizing behavior in adult relationships. ScienceDirect.
  - Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S019188691530009X

### Change and Earned Security
- Fact: Approximately 30-50% of individuals experience a shift in attachment style over time. 25-40% of adults can achieve "earned secure" attachment through therapy, meaningful relationships, or significant life experiences.
  - Source: Mindful Attachment Coaching — Can Attachment Styles Change?
  - Link: https://www.mindfulattachmentcoaching.com/blog/can-attachment-styles-change
- Fact: Earned secure adults are those who, despite inadequate care in childhood, develop secure attachment in adulthood through reflective work, therapy, or subsequent secure relationships.
  - Source: PMC — Mentalization, emotional dysregulation and attachment to alternative attachment figures in earned secure adults
  - Link: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11094461/

## Surprising / Counter-Intuitive Findings
- The distribution of attachment styles is roughly the same in adults as in infants (~65/20/15), suggesting remarkable persistence of early patterns
  - Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3572722/
- People with disorganized attachment often select partners who trigger the very attachment issues they struggle with
  - Source: https://www.relationalpsych.group/articles/disorganized-attachment-style-understanding-its-impact-and-healing
- Internal working models are not destiny — 30-50% of people shift attachment style over their lifetime
  - Source: https://www.mindfulattachmentcoaching.com/blog/can-attachment-styles-change

## Current State / Recent Breakthroughs
- Neuroimaging research now maps specific brain regions (amygdala, hippocampus, striatum, insula, cingulate) to different attachment style responses
  - Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3398354/
- The concept of "earned secure" attachment is gaining traction in clinical practice, showing that therapy and corrective relational experiences can rewire attachment patterns
  - Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11094461/

## Ethical Considerations / Controversies
- Attachment theory has been criticized for overemphasizing the mother-child bond and Western cultural norms
- Some researchers argue attachment styles are more fluid and context-dependent than categorical
- Risk of self-diagnosis and oversimplification via social media pop psychology
