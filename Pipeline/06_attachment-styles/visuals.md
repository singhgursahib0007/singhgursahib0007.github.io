- "when you reach for someone" → Character stretches both arms out toward a warm golden silhouette in the distance
- "and they reach back." → The golden silhouette reaches toward Character, hands almost touching, warm light blooming between them
- "Or don't." → The silhouette dissolves into shadow; Character's hands grasp empty air, expression falling

- "every crush," → Character blushes, holding a glowing heart-shaped balloon, another chibi silhouette in the background
- "every marriage," → Character stands under an archway of flowers beside another silhouette, rings of light floating between them
- "every breakup." → The archway crumbles; Character walks alone through falling petals, head lowered
- "The person carrying you has no idea you exist." → Wide shot: a large translucent humanoid walks forward confidently; deep inside its chest, tiny Character sits at a control panel

## Scene 2: The Experiment That Changed Everything

**[TRANSITION: Whip-pan into a 1960s-style research room]**

- "In the late 1960s, a psychologist named Mary Ainsworth" → Wide shot: a warm vintage laboratory room; Character stands by the door wearing a tiny lab coat, clipboard in hand
- "wanted to see something invisible —" → Character holds up a magnifying glass, squinting at a shimmering invisible thread between two figures
- "the bond between a mother and her baby." → Medium shot: a soft glowing tether connects a mother silhouette and a baby silhouette, Character watching from the side
- "So she designed a beautifully simple experiment" → Character sketches a diagram on a chalkboard: a room, two stick figures, arrows showing movement
- "called the Strange Situation." → Close-up of the chalkboard diagram; Character taps it proudly with a pointer
- "She put mothers and their infants in an unfamiliar room." → Wide shot: a mother silhouette and baby silhouette sit in a sparse, clean room; Character observes through a one-way mirror
- "Then she had the mother leave briefly" → The mother silhouette stands and walks toward the door; the baby watches
- "and come back. That's it." → The mother silhouette returns through the door; Character makes a simple checkmark gesture
- "What she watched was what happened at the moment of reunion." → Slow-motion close-up: the door opens, warm light spills in, the baby silhouette turns toward it

**[Beat: The Four Patterns Emerge]**

- "And what she found split the world of children" → Character stands before a prism; a single beam of white light enters and splits into four colored beams
- "into distinct categories." → The four beams land on four separate platforms, each glowing a different color
- "About 65% of the babies did what you'd expect." → Wide shot: a large group of baby silhouettes on the gold-lit platform, the biggest group
- "They got upset when mom left," → A baby silhouette sits crying, tears streaming, on the gold platform
- "and when she came back they reached for her," → The baby silhouette stretches arms up toward the returning mother silhouette
- "calmed down, and went back to playing." → The baby silhouette, now smiling, sits contentedly with building blocks; a warm golden glow surrounds them
- "Ainsworth called this secure attachment." → Character holds up the gold thread from earlier, strong and luminous
- "These babies had learned a simple, powerful rule:" → Close-up: Character's teal eyes reflect a single golden rule etched in light
- "when I need someone, someone comes." → A hand reaches down from above into frame; a tiny hand reaches up and clasps it; golden light bursts from the connection

- "But about 20% of the babies did something strange." → Cut to the blue platform; a smaller cluster of baby silhouettes sit apart, backs turned
- "When mom left, they barely reacted." → A baby silhouette sits motionless, face blank, as the mother silhouette exits
- "When she returned, they ignored her." → The mother silhouette re-enters; the baby silhouette deliberately looks away
- "Turned away. Kept playing as though nothing happened." → Close-up: the baby silhouette stacks blocks mechanically, eyes averted, cool blue light
- "Ainsworth called this avoidant attachment." → Character holds up the blue thread — it's taut and rigid, slightly frosted
- "These babies had learned a different rule:" → Character's expression is solemn, eyes reflecting a blue-tinted rule
- "when I need someone, no one comes." → A tiny hand reaches up into empty space; nothing reaches back; the frame is cold and blue
- "So I'd better stop needing." → The tiny hand slowly lowers and folds into the baby silhouette's lap; the blue light dims

- "And then there was a third group — about 15% —" → Cut to the red platform; a smaller, agitated cluster of baby silhouettes
- "who became intensely distressed when mom left," → A baby silhouette wails dramatically, arms flailing, red sparks of anxiety swirling
- "and when she came back, they clung to her desperately" → The baby silhouette latches onto the mother silhouette's leg with both arms
- "but also pushed her away." → Same baby silhouette shoves the mother silhouette with one hand while gripping with the other
- "They couldn't be soothed." → The mother silhouette tries to comfort the baby; red agitation lines still pulse around the baby
- "Ainsworth called this anxious attachment." → Character holds up the red thread — it vibrates and pulses erratically
- "Their rule was the cruelest one:" → Close-up: Character winces, teal eyes reflecting flickering red light
- "sometimes someone comes, sometimes they don't." → Split frame: left side shows a hand reaching down, right side shows empty space — alternating, flickering
- "I can never be sure." → The baby silhouette looks back and forth frantically between the two flickering halves
- "So I'd better never stop reaching." → The baby silhouette reaches upward endlessly, arms outstretched, red glow intensifying

- "Years later, researchers identified a fourth pattern," → Cut to the grey platform, dim and cracked; Character approaches cautiously
- "found in about 5 to 10% of children," → A very small cluster of baby silhouettes on the fractured grey platform, huddled
- "almost always linked to trauma, abuse," → Dark storm clouds gather above the grey platform; jagged lightning in the background
- "or a caregiver who was simultaneously the source of comfort and terror." → A single silhouette flickers between warm golden glow and dark menacing shadow, alternating rapidly
- "It's called disorganized attachment," → Character holds up the grey thread — it's tangled, knotted, fraying in multiple directions
- "and these children didn't have a coherent strategy at all." → A baby silhouette starts walking one direction, stops, turns, stops again, frozen mid-step
- "They'd approach their mother and then freeze." → The baby silhouette walks toward the mother silhouette, then locks up entirely, arms stiff at its sides
- "Walk toward her and then fall to the floor." → The baby silhouette takes a step forward and collapses, grey static flickering around it
- "Their rule was the most devastating:" → Close-up of Character's face, eyes wide with empathy, grey light reflected
- "the person I need is the person I'm afraid of." → A single silhouette simultaneously radiates warmth from one hand and casts a dark shadow from the other; a tiny figure cowers between them

- "Four patterns. Written early." → Wide overhead shot: the four colored platforms glow — gold, blue, red, grey — with Character standing at the center
- "And here's what nobody expected —" → Character looks up with surprise; the four threads in its hand begin stretching forward into the distance
- "they don't go away." → The threads extend into an infinite horizon; Character watches them disappear into adulthood silhouettes far ahead

## Scene 3: The Software That Keeps Running

**[TRANSITION: The four threads pull Character forward through a time tunnel into the adult world]**

- "In 1987, psychologists Cindy Hazan and Phillip Shaver" → Character lands in a bright university office, two researcher silhouettes at a desk covered in papers
- "asked a deceptively simple question:" → Character tilts its head, a large glowing question mark hovering above
- "what if romantic love is just attachment all over again?" → Character holds a baby rattle in one hand and a rose in the other; they morph into the same object
- "What if your partner is basically the adult version" → Split screen: a caregiver silhouette on the left, a romantic partner silhouette on the right, mirroring the same pose
- "of the caregiver you reached for as a baby?" → The two silhouettes overlap and merge into one glowing figure

- "They ran studies and found something remarkable." → Character's eyes widen as it unrolls a long scroll of data that cascades to the floor
- "The distribution of attachment styles in adults" → Wide shot: two identical pie charts float side by side — one labeled with a baby icon, one with an adult icon
- "was almost identical to what Ainsworth had found in infants." → The two pie charts slide together and overlap perfectly, colors matching
- "Same percentages. Same patterns." → Close-up: the merged chart pulses; gold 65%, blue 20%, red 15%
- "Roughly 65% of adults were securely attached." → The gold section glows warmly
- "About 20% were avoidant." → The blue section frosts over
- "About 15% were anxious." → The red section pulses
- "As if the original code had simply been copied and pasted into adulthood," → Character watches a glowing line of light duplicate itself, the copy drifting upward into an adult silhouette
- "with only the names of the characters changed." → The baby silhouettes from earlier morph smoothly into adult-sized silhouettes, same colors, same postures

- "John Bowlby, the father of attachment theory," → Character stands beside a large framed portrait silhouette, respectful posture
- "had predicted this." → The portrait silhouette points forward knowingly; Character nods
- "He called the mechanism 'internal working models' —" → Character peers into a glowing architectural blueprint floating in midair, showing a small house-like structure inside a head silhouette
- "mental blueprints, formed in infancy," → Macro shot: tiny glowing blueprint lines etch themselves into a baby-sized brain shape
- "about whether you're worthy of love" → Character looks at its own reflection in a mirror; the reflection glows warmly or dims uncertainly
- "and whether other people can be trusted." → Character extends a hand toward a shadowy figure; a bridge of light either forms or crumbles between them
- "These models don't sit in your conscious mind" → Character tries to grab at floating thoughts above its head but passes right through them
- "where you can examine them." → Character squints and reaches again; the thoughts dissolve like mist
- "They live deeper, in the automatic reflexes" → Cut to interior view: deep inside a body silhouette, gears and pulleys operate on their own in dim light
- "of how you respond when someone gets close," → A figure approaches Character; Character's body reacts before its face changes expression — arms open or close involuntarily
- "or when someone pulls away." → A figure steps back from Character; Character's hands twitch forward automatically

- "And your brain literally wires itself around them." → Wide macro shot: glowing neural pathways form and solidify around the four colored threads inside a translucent brain
- "Neuroimaging studies show that avoidantly attached people" → Character wearing the blue thread watches a brain scan image where certain regions are dimmed
- "have reduced emotional processing in social situations —" → The emotional center of the brain dims to near-black while social figures pass by
- "their brains have actually learned to dial down feelings of connection." → Character turns a large dimmer switch; warm light fades to cold blue
- "Anxiously attached people show the opposite:" → Character wearing the red thread watches a brain scan where regions blaze bright
- "hyperactivation, especially in the amygdala," → Macro close-up: a small almond-shaped structure deep in the brain pulses red-hot like an alarm beacon
- "the brain's threat detector." → The almond-shaped structure sends out radar-like waves; Character flinches with each pulse
- "For them, love and danger are processed through the same circuitry." → A heart and a lightning bolt travel down the same glowing neural pathway, merging
- "The attachment style isn't just a personality quirk." → Character stands small before a vast internal architecture of neural pathways
- "It's architecture." → Wide pullback: the neural pathways form the scaffolding of an entire building around Character

## Scene 4: What the Styles Look Like in the Wild

**[TRANSITION: The neural architecture dissolves into a real-world cityscape; Character steps onto a busy street]**

- "So what does this actually look like" → Character stands at a crossroads in a vibrant town, looking around curiously
- "when you're an adult trying to love another adult?" → Two adult silhouettes face each other awkwardly; Character watches from a park bench

**[Beat: Secure]**

- "If you're securely attached, relationships feel relatively natural." → Character walks hand-in-hand with a companion through a sunlit park, golden glow, relaxed posture
- "You can ask for help without shame." → Character openly extends a hand toward its companion, smiling; the companion takes it easily
- "You can handle disagreements without catastrophizing." → Character and companion face each other with mild frowns, a small grey cloud between them that stays small
- "When your partner pulls away briefly," → The companion steps back a few paces; Character watches calmly
- "you feel a pinch, not a panic." → Close-up: Character touches its chest with a slight wince, but its expression remains steady
- "You assume the best until proven otherwise." → Character looks at the companion stepping away and imagines a warm golden thought bubble showing the companion returning

**[Beat: Avoidant]**

- "If you're avoidantly attached, closeness triggers a quiet alarm." → Character stands as a companion approaches; a small blue alarm bell rings silently above Character's head
- "You might say things like 'I need my space' a lot," → Character holds both hands up in a gentle stop gesture, a blue force field shimmering around it
- "not because you don't care," → Inside the force field, Character's heart glows warmly — it does care
- "but because intimacy feels like a trap" → The companion's approaching warmth morphs into walls closing in; Character looks claustrophobic
- "your nervous system learned to escape." → Character squeezes through a gap in the closing walls, breathing hard
- "You might keep people at arm's length" → Character holds a yardstick-length glowing rod between itself and others, maintaining exact distance
- "and then wonder why your relationships feel empty." → Character sits alone in a large, cold, blue-tinted room, looking at the empty space around it
- "You associate love with losing yourself." → Character's reflection in a puddle dissolves as a heart-shape ripple passes through it

**[Beat: Anxious]**

- "If you're anxiously attached, distance triggers that alarm instead." → Character watches a companion walk a few steps away; a massive red alarm blares above Character's head
- "A partner who doesn't text back" → Character stares at a blank glowing rectangle in its hands, no response appearing
- "becomes evidence of abandonment." → The blank rectangle morphs into a giant red exit door slamming shut
- "You might engage in what psychologists call 'protest behaviors' —" → Character waves arms frantically, jumps up and down, tries to pull companion back with a red tether
- "picking fights, making jealous comments, threatening to leave —" → Rapid montage: Character points accusingly, crosses arms, turns away dramatically — all in red-tinted frames
- "all as desperate, unconscious attempts to pull your partner back." → Behind all the frantic actions, a ghostly overlay shows tiny Character inside, just reaching out with both hands
- "You know it's destructive." → Character pauses mid-gesture, looking at the chaos around it with dawning awareness
- "You can't stop." → Character's hands move again on their own, pulled by red puppet strings from below
- "The ancient software is running." → Macro shot: deep inside Character's chest, old gears grind and spin, glowing red

**[Beat: Disorganized]**

- "And if your attachment is disorganized," → Character stands in a fractured grey space, the ground cracked beneath its feet
- "you're caught in a loop that doesn't resolve." → Character walks in a tight circle, footprints overlapping, grey static buzzing
- "You want closeness desperately" → Character lunges toward a warm glowing companion silhouette
- "and then sabotage it the moment it arrives." → The instant Character's hand touches the companion, Character's other hand shoves them away
- "You might choose partners who are unavailable, even harmful," → Character reaches toward shadowy figures that flicker between warm and menacing
- "because chaos feels more familiar than safety." → Split screen: left shows a stormy, familiar landscape; right shows a calm meadow that Character eyes with suspicion
- "Research shows that this pattern often mediates the link" → Character stands at the center of a chain: childhood storm clouds on one end, fractured adult relationships on the other
- "between childhood trauma and the most destructive relationship dynamics in adulthood." → The chain glows grey, connecting the storm to the fractured ground

- "These patterns aren't character flaws." → Character looks at all four colored threads laid out before it; none are broken or defective — just different
- "They're survival strategies." → Character as a tiny child stands in a vast dark landscape, holding one glowing thread like a lifeline
- "Brilliant adaptations by a small child" → Close-up: baby-sized Character ties a thread into a sturdy knot with determination
- "who was doing the best they could with what they had." → Wide shot: tiny Character sits in a sparse room with only one small light source, using it to illuminate everything it can

## Scene 5: The Code Can Be Rewritten

**[TRANSITION: A sunrise breaks over the horizon behind Character; warm light floods in]**

- "Here's where this story turns." → Character stops walking, turns around to face the camera, a sunrise glowing behind it
- "Because the most important finding in attachment research might be this one:" → Character holds up a single glowing scroll, eyes wide with hope
- "the patterns can change." → The four colored threads in Character's hand begin to shimmer and shift colors, the rigid ones softening

- "Psychologists call it 'earned secure' attachment." → Character carefully polishes a gold thread that was previously blue/red/grey, and it begins to glow warmly
- "It describes people who grew up insecurely attached" → Flashback silhouette: small Character clutching a frayed colored thread in a dim room
- "but managed, through therapy," → Character sits across from a kind companion silhouette in a warm room, talking openly
- "through a transformative relationship," → Character walks beside a steady companion; their joined hands glow and warmth spreads up Character's arm
- "through the slow, patient work of making sense of their own story," → Character sits at a desk, carefully untangling a knotted thread, lamp burning warmly nearby
- "to shift toward security." → The thread in Character's hands gradually turns gold; Character smiles softly
- "Studies suggest that somewhere between 30 and 50% of people" → Wide shot: a crowd of silhouettes; roughly a third to half begin glowing gold from within
- "experience a meaningful change in attachment style over their lifetime." → Time-lapse: the gold glow spreads slowly through the crowd like sunrise
- "Not because they forget the past," → Character looks back at a shadowy doorway behind it — the past is still visible
- "but because they build a new relationship with it." → Character places a warm golden frame around the shadowy doorway, acknowledging it with a gentle nod

- "The key ingredient seems to be something deceptively simple:" → Character holds up a single golden key, turning it thoughtfully
- "coherence." → The key fits into a lock; the tangled threads behind Character straighten into a clear, organized pattern
- "Earned-secure adults can talk about their childhood —" → Character sits in a warm circle of light, gesturing openly, calm expression
- "even painful parts — with balance and clarity." → Character holds a dark orb (representing pain) steadily in both hands without dropping it or being consumed by it
- "They don't minimize the hurt" → Character does not shrink the dark orb — it stays its real size
- "and they don't drown in it." → Character does not let the dark orb grow to engulf it — it remains held, contained
- "They've developed what researchers call reflective functioning:" → Character looks into a calm pool of water and sees not just its reflection but also the reflection of its parents' silhouettes behind it
- "the ability to understand that their parents were people," → The parent silhouettes in the reflection gain depth and detail — they have their own small threads trailing behind them
- "flawed and shaped by their own histories," → The parent silhouettes' threads extend backward into their own childhoods, a chain of generations
- "and that the patterns passed down are not the final word." → Character gently sets down the chain of threads, then picks up a fresh golden thread of its own

- "Your attachment style was written before you had any say in the matter." → Baby-sized Character sits passively as glowing code writes itself around it
- "But knowing the code is running —" → Adult Character notices the code running on the walls around it, eyes focusing with awareness
- "actually seeing it —" → Character traces the glowing lines with its finger, mapping them, understanding
- "is the first step to editing it." → Character carefully adjusts one line of the glowing code; it shifts from blue/red/grey toward gold
- "The child who learned that no one comes when they cry" → Small flashback: baby Character crying alone in a dim room, no one at the door
- "doesn't have to keep living by that rule at thirty-five." → Adult Character stands in the same room, but now opens the door itself, light flooding in
- "The patterns are old." → Character looks at the original threads — they're worn, faded, ancient-looking
- "But you are not the child who wrote them." → Split frame: baby Character on the left, adult Character on the right, clearly different, clearly grown
- "You're the adult who can choose to rewrite them —" → Adult Character sits at a glowing workbench, carefully weaving a new golden thread
- "slowly, imperfectly, but genuinely." → Close-up of Character's hands weaving — the thread isn't perfectly smooth, it has small knots and texture, but it glows warm gold
